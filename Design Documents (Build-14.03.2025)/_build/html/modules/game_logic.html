Game Logic Module

This module contains the game logic functions and classes that drive the game mechanics, including the core GameLogic class that manages the rules and flow of the Property Tycoon game.

Key Features

  • Game State Management: Tracks players, properties, bank funds, and game progression

  • Turn Processing: Handles player turns, dice rolls, and movement around the board

  • Property Transactions: Manages buying, selling, mortgaging, and developing properties

  • Card Handling: Implements Pot Luck and Opportunity Knocks card effects

  • Money Management: Controls financial transactions between players and the bank

  • Auction System: Provides a complete property auction mechanism

  • Bankruptcy Handling: Manages player bankruptcy and asset liquidation

  • AI Player Logic: Integrates with AI player decision-making systems

  • Game Rules Enforcement: Ensures all game actions follow the established rules

  • Voluntary Exit System: Handles players who choose to leave the game early

  • Time-Limited Mode: Supports abridged game format with time limits

  • Free Parking System: Manages the accumulation and collection of Free Parking funds

Game Constants

The GameLogic class defines several important constants:

  • BANK_LIMIT: The total amount of money in the bank (50,000)

  • MAX_HOUSES_PER_PROPERTY: Maximum number of houses per property (4)

  • MAX_HOTELS_PER_PROPERTY: Maximum number of hotels per property (1)

  • GAME_TOKENS: Available player tokens (boot, smartphone, ship, hatstand, cat, iron)

Game Initialization

The GameLogic class initializes the game state with:

  • Empty player list

  • Bank money set to BANK_LIMIT

  • Free parking fund set to 0

  • Property data loaded from external source

  • Shuffled card decks

  • Game state tracking variables

  • AI difficulty selection (easy or hard)

  • Completed circuits tracking

  • Jail free card tracking

Card Systems

The module defines two types of card decks:

  1. Pot Luck Cards: Cards that provide various effects like collecting money, paying fines, or moving to specific locations.

  2. Opportunity Knocks Cards: Similar to Pot Luck cards but with different effects and probabilities.

Each card contains text to display to the player and an action function that implements its effect on the game state. Card effects include:

  • Collecting or paying money

  • Moving to specific board locations

  • Getting out of jail

  • Paying repair costs based on property development

  • Collecting money from other players

  • Making choices (like paying a fine or drawing another card)

The card system supports: * Get Out of Jail Free cards: Players can hold these cards for future use * Repair assessment cards: Calculate costs based on houses and hotels owned * Collection cards: Collect money from all other players (e.g., birthday card) * Movement cards: Move to specific locations or relative positions

Property Management

The GameLogic class provides comprehensive property management:

  • Property Purchase: Players can buy properties they land on

  • Property Auction: When a player declines to buy a property, it goes to auction

  • Property Development: Players can build houses and hotels on properties

  • Mortgaging: Properties can be mortgaged for immediate cash

  • Rent Calculation: Dynamic rent calculation based on property development

  • Group Completion: Special handling for completing property sets

  • Development Validation: Ensures houses are built evenly across color groups

  • Selling Buildings: Players can sell houses and hotels to raise funds

  • Property Transfer: Handles ownership changes during auctions and bankruptcy

Turn Processing

The GameLogic class handles the complete turn sequence:

  1. Dice Rolling: Generates random dice values

  2. Movement: Updates player position based on dice roll

  3. Space Handling: Processes the effect of the space the player lands on

  4. Property Actions: Handles property purchases, rent payments, etc.

  5. Special Space Actions: Manages effects of special spaces like Go, Jail, Free Parking, etc.

  6. Card Drawing: Processes card effects when landing on card spaces

  7. Doubles Handling: Special logic for rolling doubles (extra turn or jail)

  8. Circuit Completion: Tracks and rewards players for completing a lap around the board

  9. AI Turn Automation: Automatically processes AI player turns

Jail System

The game implements a comprehensive jail system:

  • Going to Jail: Players go to jail by landing on the “Go to Jail” space, drawing a card that sends them to jail, or rolling three consecutive doubles.

  • Jail Status Tracking: The player’s jail status is tracked via the ‘in_jail’ and ‘jail_turns’ properties.

  • Leaving Jail Options: * Rolling doubles (automatic release) * Using a “Get Out of Jail Free” card * Paying a £50 fine * Staying in jail (new strategic option)

  • Forced Release: After three turns in jail, players must pay the £50 fine or go bankrupt.

  • Jail Limitations: Players in jail cannot collect rent or buy properties.

  • AI Jail Strategy: AI players have specific strategies for handling jail based on their current financial situation and difficulty level.

Financial System

The game implements a comprehensive financial system:

  • Bank Transactions: Methods for paying to and from the bank

  • Player Transactions: Direct money transfers between players

  • Transaction Validation: Ensures all transactions are valid

  • Bankruptcy Handling: Manages player bankruptcy and asset liquidation

  • Payment Handling: Special methods for handling various types of payments, including: * Regular payments to the bank * Payments to Free Parking * Birthday collections from all players * Property repair assessments

  • Bankruptcy Prevention: AI strategies to avoid bankruptcy by selling properties/houses

  • Property Value Calculation: Methods to calculate total player assets

Free Parking System

The game implements a Free Parking pot that accumulates money:

  • Fund Tracking: Keeps track of the total amount in the Free Parking fund

  • Fund Collection: Players landing on Free Parking collect the accumulated funds

  • Fund Sources: * Jail fines (£50) * Tax space payments * Fines from cards * Penalties from certain game events

  • Automatic Updates: The fund amount is displayed on the board and updated real-time

  • Integration with Payments: Financial transactions automatically contribute to the fund when appropriate

Auction System

When a player declines to buy a property, it goes to auction:

  1. Auction Setup: Creates an auction context with property information and eligible players

  2. Bidding Mechanism: Players can place bids or pass their turn

  3. Eligibility Rules: Players must have completed at least one circuit of the board to participate

  4. AI Bidding Logic: AI players evaluate property value and make strategic bids

  5. Time Limits: Auctions have a time limit for each bidder to make a decision

  6. Winner Determination: The highest bidder gets the property after all others pass or time expires

  7. Visual Interface: Full auction UI with bidder information, current price, and timer

  8. Auction Completion: Property transfer and payment processing after auction ends

AI Player Integration

The GameLogic class integrates with AI player logic:

  • AI Decision Making: Handles AI player decisions during their turn

  • Property Purchase Decisions: AI evaluates whether to buy properties

  • Auction Bidding: AI determines appropriate bid amounts

  • Bankruptcy Prevention: AI strategizes to avoid bankruptcy

  • Property Development: AI decides when to build houses/hotels

  • Mortgage Management: AI decides when to mortgage or unmortgage properties

  • Jail Strategy: AI decides optimal jail strategy based on game situation

  • AI Difficulty Levels: Supports both easy and hard AI implementations with different strategies

  • Emotional Decision Making: Hard AI incorporates emotional state into decisions

Game Modes and End Conditions

The GameLogic class supports multiple game modes:

  • Full Game: Traditional game mode that ends when only one player remains solvent

  • Abridged Game: Time-limited mode that ends after a specified duration

  • Asset Calculation: In abridged mode, winner is determined by total assets including property values

  • Circuit Completion: Tracks how many times each player has completed a circuit of the board

  • Final Lap System: When time expires in abridged mode, players complete their current lap

Voluntary Exit System

The GameLogic class allows players to exit the game voluntarily:

  • Exit Processing: Special handling when players choose to leave the game

  • Property Liquidation: Returns all owned properties to the bank

  • Player Tracking: Maintains records of voluntarily exited players separately from bankrupted players

  • State Management: Updates game state to exclude exited players from turns

  • Asset Calculation: Records final assets of voluntarily exited players for end-game calculations
